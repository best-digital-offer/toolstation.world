Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between Title Case and Capitalize Each Word?

Title Case follows standard capitalization rules for titles, typically not capitalizing articles (a, an, the), short prepositions (in, on, at), and conjunctions (and, but, or) unless they're the first or last word. Capitalize Each Word capitalizes every single word regardless of its grammatical function.

Does the tool preserve formatting and punctuation?

Yes! Our converter only changes letter capitalization while preserving all other formatting including spaces, line breaks, punctuation marks, numbers, and special characters. Your text structure remains completely intact.

Can I convert large amounts of text?

Absolutely! There's no limit to the amount of text you can convert. The tool handles everything from single words to entire documents, processing them instantly regardless of length.

Is my text stored or saved anywhere?

No, your text is completely private. All conversions happen in your browser using JavaScript - no data is sent to our servers or stored anywhere. You can use the tool with complete confidence for sensitive content.

Does this work for languages other than English?

Yes! The tool works with any language that uses uppercase and lowercase letters, including accented characters. It properly handles international characters and maintains language-specific formatting rules.
